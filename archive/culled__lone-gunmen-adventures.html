The TOP SECRET details of the (first) X-Files spin-off series,

THE ADVENTURES OF THE LONE GUNMEN

Regular Cast:

Frohike (Tom Braidwood)
Byers (Bruce Harwood)
Langly (Dean Haglund)


EPISODE GUIDE: [excerpts]

1: Pilot.

Against the wishes of his fellow Gunmen, Frohike places an ad in
several national newspapers:

IF THERE'S SOMETHING STRANGE IN YOUR NEIGHBOURHOOD
WHO YOU GONNA CALL?
YEAH, WELL, WHY NOT TRY THE LONE GUNMEN INSTEAD?
WE DO ANYTHING ANYTIME
(PROVIDED IT'S WEIRD)
(BABES ESPECIALLY WELCOME: PLEASE ASK ABOUT OUR SPECIAL PAYMENT PLAN)

Will their first case, involving a beautiful diver, a school of
three-headed fish, artefacts of an unknown aquatic civilisation, and

a brutal cover-up by Men-in-Black-Scuba-Gear, also be their last?

4: The Lone Gunmen of the Apocalypse

Is televangelist Micky Tayker using subliminal messages to extort
millions from his gullible audience? When militant atheist Byers
sends Tayker the Gunmens' life savings, Langly and Frohike
investigate. But can they stay away from the TV long enough to avoid

indoctrination by Tayker's ubiquitous commercials? As the new series

of Doctor Who approaches, they realise time is running out...

8: Frohike In Love (Part 1)

_The Lone Gunmen_'s first female subscriber turns out to be weirder
than Frohike's wildest fantasies. Will they do the 'wild thing'
before Frohike learns the truth about her past?

9: Amour Fou... D'oh! (Part 2)

Could Flower of the Desert ("please, call me 'Flo'") really be
Frohike's long-lost daughter, the product of a drug/sex orgy in the
sixties? Only a DNA test will tell... but why does Flo's DNA have
*three* strands? Is this trioxyribonucleic acid a natural mutation;
evidence of alien abduction; or a result of the psychedelic cocktail
that accompanied her conception? Could it be that one of Frohike's
hippy friends was really an incognito EBE? ("I guess it coulda been
that guy with the two heads...") And who is the tall dark
shape-shifter who seems to lurk in every shadow?

20: The Owls Are Not What They Seem

A shared dream directs the Gunmen to the sleepy logging town of Twin

Peaks, where they join FBI Special Agent Dale Cooper in the hunt for

an arboreal serial killer. Can they catch 'Killer Bobcat' before
another tree dies?

24: Smells Like Unquiet Spirit (Part 1)

Asked by a beautiful widow to investigate the suicide of her pop
star husband, the Gunmen start their own grunge band.
The dead man's grave is empty, and he was seen in several
local
supermarkets shortly after his funeral. Mysterious letters, signed
with his name, have arrived every Tuesday since his death.
More importantly, will chart success bring our heroes'
search for Truth to an untimely end? How do you keep secrets from
the Men in Black, when three unauthorised biographies come out every

week, and updates appear daily in the supermarket tabloids? Should
they care? After all, they have groupies now...
The season ends with a bang, when the Gunmen's Mystery Machine is
seen hitting a tree, and then bursting into flame. Is this the end?

Season 2

1: Light My Fire (Part 2)

The Gunmen, dressed as Hell's Angels, watch the burning van from
behind a tree, before hopping on their Harleys (bought with their
last royalties) and heading for Las Vegas, where Nirvain's letters
were postmarked. There, posing as Lone Gunmen Impersonators, they
continue the investigation. But could the great man *really* be
dead? In this crazy old world, anything's possible... But why does
that really bad sixty-year-old Elvis impersonator refuse to take a
DNA test? And who is the mysterious proprietor of the Jim Morrison
Hotel?
