  And now, Maxwell/Intensity Enterprises present:
  
  THE PENGUIN REPORT
  
  Screenplay by Annette Fraser from an original idea by Intensity.
  
  All rights reserved.
  
  
  "Death is not an option," she hisses.
  
  It is a dark room.  The lights are out; the room lit only by the flickering
  of candles.  The two are hunched over a table, looking at each other
  intently, their faces sombre in the eerie illumination.
  
  Silence fills the air.
  
  She speaks again.  "Choose, and choose now."
  
  Frowning, he shifts his gaze from her face.  "But it's too difficult.  You
  know how it is."  Plaintively, he lifts his eyes to hers.
  
  "No."  She thumps the table with her fist causing the other to flinch.
  "End this charade now and tell me if you want tea or coffee."
  
  Abruptly, the lights flick back on showing the room to be a kitchen.  "Ah!"
  says the woman, pushing back her chair.  "Well, I'm sick of this, you're
  having tea."  She stands.  "Do you want sugar?"
  
  "Er, yeah," replies her companion.
  
  "Milk?"
  
  "Yeah again."
  
  She moves towards the fridge and as she opens it she steps back in
  surprise.  A man in pink evening dress emerges from the appliance saying,
  "Whenever life gets you down, Mrs Brown,
  And things seem hard or tough
  And people are stupid, obnoxious or daft
  And you feel that you've had quite enough..."
  
  The man at the table leaps to his feet and shouts out, "Annette!  Stop him!
   He's going to sing!"
  
  The man in pink begins to sing.
  
  "Just remember that you're standing on a planet that's evolving and
  revolving..."
  
  "Oh, shut up!" says Annette, hitting him on the head with the kettle from
  the kitchen bench.  "Now to business."
  
  Her companion once again seats himself at the table and raises an imaginary
  glass.  "To business!" he says heartily.
  
  "Oh now you shut up Intensity," scowls Annette, "we've got to get an awards
  ceremony happening.  What brilliant ideas have you had so far?"
  
  Intensity raises his eyebrows and shrugs.  "Erm, well, there's not much to
  work with with the 'Petrified Penguin Awards' unless I can draw an ASCII
  penguin for them."  Suddenly inspired, he begins looking around the room.
  "Yeah!  Where's that penguin of yours, Annette?  I need something to work
  from."
  
  "He's on the television where all good penguins are at 8:14.  Oh hell, he's
  only got five seconds before he's due to explode.  Quick!"
  
  They rush into the lounge room but they're too late! A cheerful voice from
  the television announces, "Good evening.  It's now 8:15 and time for the
  penguin on top of your television set to explode."
  
  Immediately, the penguin explodes violently, releasing a flock of smaller
  penguins which, after righting themselves, begin to waddle self-importantly
  around the room.
  
  "Never fear Annette," intones Intensity with a rousing fanfare and a
  flourish, "I shall catch them for you."
  
  Intensity begins to run after the nearest penguin, catching it easily and
  tucking it under his left arm.  Pausing only to shout a pained "Oh, my
  knee," he continues in his quest.
  
  Meanwhile, there is a knock at the door.  Ignoring Intensity's frantic
  scrabbling, Annette opens it and a horde of unwashed ATXFers floods into
  the room.  Annette casts an evil glance at Intensity who continues chasing
  the penguins, the second one proving to be much harder to catch than the
  first.
  
  Sighing, she directs her attention to the gathered masses (what better way
  to describe the heap of X-Files-starved flesh crammed onto her couch?) who
  are staring at her with breathless expectation.  She forces a smile.
  
  "Welcome, one and all, to the inaugural Petrified Penguin Awards.  Make
  yourselves comfortable and relax."
  
  She takes a grubby sheet of paper from her pocket and begins to read.
  
  "The IF YOU WON’T PLAY MY WAY I'M TAKING MY BALL AND GOING HOME AWARD  goes
  to Steve Leahy for suggesting I make up my own award:
                                  Why not make up your own award... say, the "Sto
  ne(d) Penguin? :-)
  And then, when I did, saying it wasn’t suitable:
                                  > Petrified Penguins
           ^^^^^^^^^
                                  Nah, it's gotta be "Stone(d)", not petrified...
  
  Well Steve, sucks to you and here's your Petrified Penguin."
  
  Intensity rushes to Steve and hands him a small penguin.  As Steve
  tentatively takes the wriggling bird, it immediately stops writhing and
  stiffens, thrusting its flippers and feet out in a crude approximation of
  an X.  There are murmurs of wonder and approval from the crowd of ATXFers.
  Gene and Ingrid produce laptops, calculators and slide rules and start
  trying to discover the scientific explanation for the penguin's odd
  behaviour.  Annette and Intensity smile conspiratorially and Intensity goes
  back to chasing penguins.
  
  'The ANSETT FREQUENT FLYERS SOCIAL BUTTERFLY AWARD is presented to Nina
  Notomi for managing to (and *wanting* to) attend two gatherings, in
  different states, on consecutive weekends."
  
  Immediately howls of protest emanate from somewhere in the crowd.  "But I
  was the first!"  yells an indignant voice.  "I did it first!  I went to
  Brisbane first!  Sure, it wasn't on consecutive weekends, but I did it!
  You try being 6'5' and spending 14 hours on a bus. That's real sacrifice."
  Annette and Intensity go into a huddle and there is much raising of voices
  and wild gesticulation.
  
  "Ok ok,"  says Annette straightening up.  "Just this once, as we happen to
  have a couple extra, you can have a Petrified Penguin for being THE FIRST
  TO ATTEND AN INTERSTATE GATHERING."  She pelts the penguin at Matt Curr who
  gathers it up lovingly, a huge and foolish grin on his face.
  
  Intensity kneels reverently before Nina and bows his head as he shyly hands
  her her penguin.  She smiles beatifically and, as their eyes meet, he
  faints from an excess of rapture and falls to the ground in front of her,
  twitching.  Both Matt's and Nina's penguins immediately go through the same
  transformation as Steve's.
  
  As Annette continues to announce awards, Intensity gradually comes to,
  shakes his head and, with a sideways glance at Nina, sets off to round up
  more penguins.
  
  "The WEBSTER'S DICTIONARY TO COIN A PHRASE AWARD goes to none other than
  Antti Roppola for his famous post (now enshrined in the FAQ):
                                  Have we just witnessed the creation of a new at
  x acronym?
                                  I like the idea of "Annette was looking pretty
  BP the other night      when
          she
                          saw the new Rolling Stone", or "I was feeling BP when I
          tried to
  follow the
                          Muppet thread".
                                  BP      - To be or appear to be confused and da
  zed by surrounding events.
  
  And judging from the looks on most of your faces I would say that we’re all
  feeling a little BP at the moment."
  
  Intensity lobs Antti's penguin to him who makes a perfect outfield catch.
  
  "Hugh!" screams Annette, "For goodness sake, sit still and stop taking
  photos!"  Suitably chastised, Hugh meekly resumes his seat.
  
  'The BILLY GRAHAM I'LL BELIEVE ANYTHING TELL ME WHERE TO SEND MY MONEY
  AWARD is of course awarded to Ralph, the Wonder Llama, for the following
  admission:
             Did a religion start in my absence ?
             Please, please tell me what I must do to attain BP status, to
  please
  the
           BP.
             I'm going up north next week, I shall sit at his feet and desire
            enlightenment of him.
  
  "Especially as he has provided us with photographic evidence of said
  activity.
  
  "Intensity, this is all taking too long, just throw their penguins to
  them," scowls Annette as Intensity prepares for another dramatic
  presentation.  Hanging his head, he does as he is bidden and Ralph manages
  to catch his penguin in his mouth.
  
  "The KIRSTY WEATHERHOG MEMORIAL GOLD EXCLAMATION MARK is....um, is...."
  Annette frowns and looks up as a strangled choking noise and then the sound
  of loud, uncontrollable sobbing fills the room.  “Would someone hand $teve
  a box of tissues," she says with a resigned sigh.
  
  "Anyway," she continues,  "this award is not being presented this week as
  there have been no worthy posts.  Come on girls, you’re slipping here."
  Holly and Alicia glare at each other and each sets her jaw with grim
  determination.
  
  "The SIGMUND FREUD THAT'S A NICE SLIP AWARD is presented to Daniel Lam for
  the following revelation from his psyche:

  to be
     a potential killer".
  
            I hereby nominate myself.  I have always hated potentials,    especia
  lly
  ones
     which lead to complicated wave functions.  I have    wanted to kill a
  potential
     for a long time, but haven't got around      to it yet.  What more of a
  potential
     potential killer do you want?
  
  "The DON'T MAKE ME LAUGH YOU'VE GOT TO BE KIDDING AWARD goes to Lyndal
  Cairns for this post:
            If anyone has posted a message to me on the newsgroup and are
  waiting
  for
     my reply - sorry.
  
  "The DEAD SET LEGEND AWARD FOR MOST IRRITATING POST BY A DELURKER/NEWBIE
  is presented to to Colin Baskin for his...um....interesting response to
  Nina which began thusly:
             While you're probably sick of hearing about Nina Notomi,it is crucia

     (The rest of the letter is omitted due to time constraints and so as not

     offend your collective sensibilities)

  "Steve Leahy has also won the MOST VALIANT DEFENDER OF THE PRINCIPLE OF
  RELEVANT SUBJECTS; AND CHANGING THE SUBJECT HEADER WHEN THE THREAD CHANGES
  AWARD, incidentally, the only nomination this week to have both a nominator
  AND a seconder.
  
  "And now for the final award for this evening.  The SIGNIFICANT AWARD is
  shared tonight and the recipients are (drum roll please)... Intensity and
  me!"
  
  Howls of protest erupt from the throng and cries of "cheats, it's rigged"
  can be heard.
  
  "Enough!" snarls Annette, brandishing blood red talons and nasty big pointy
  teeth.  "It's *my* awards ceremony and I'll do what I want to.  Intensity
  gets the award for that fabulous portrait of Steve Leahy and I earned it
  with that penguin story you all liked so much.  So nyah."
  
  Grumbling, the group settles down again as there is a knock at the door.
  
  "The pizza's here," yells Marcus.  "I don't suppose you have any topped
  with camembert do you?" he politely asks Dougie, the delivery boy.
  
  Ten minutes later, mangled pizza boxes cover the floor, cheese and olives
  can be found sticking to the ceiling and on the couch is a half empty two
  litre bottle of coke pumping out the remainder of its contents as it lies
  on its side.  The penguins have all been presented and a strange silence
  has fallen over the room.
  
  Only three people are left, the others having gorged themselves and
  mysteriously vanished back from whence they came leaving the cleaning up
  behind them.  Intensity sits in the corner feeding anchovies to his penguin
  and thinking of Nina.  Annette lets her gaze roam around the battlefield
  until it alights on the third body.  It is Matt Curr, fossicking through
  the boxes and rabidly finishing off as many pieces of pizza as he can find,
  singing snatches of 'Happy Feet' as he goes.
  
  "Well, this is it Matt," says Annette.  "I guess I'm just going to have to
  interview you."
  
  1.  What is your age?
  22 on a good day, on a birthday just add one.
  
  2.  What was your first X-Files episode?
  Umm.... the Pilot, first time it was shown. Yes, I'm one of those original
  Thursday night people.
  
  3.  What is your favourite X-Files episode?
  It's tough. "Duane Barry" is great because it shows Mulder doing real FBI
  stuff, "Ice" because it's such a great story and packed with suspense (a la
  "The Thing"), but my favourite has to be "Humbug". The jokes are hilarious,
  the irony superb, and basically the episode's just very surreal.
  
  4.  What is your least favourite X-Files episode?
  I haven't seen "Firewalker” yet, so I'd have to say "Fire”. It had some
  good lines and was interesting, but it was pretty boring. Guess I'm just
  disappointed because they could have done it a lot better. Also I thought
  "One Breath" was pretty disappointing. About the only good thing in that
  was the Mulder vs Cancerman scene.
  
  5.  Why do you like The X-Files?
  I need to drool occasionally. Story, script, acting, music, direction, plus
  you never know what the hell's going to happen.
  
  6.  What computer do you use?
  The Mighty SuperGrover, the funkiest Power Mac 8100/110 on the planet.
  
  7.  If you were Mulder and you found Scully naked, tied up and smeared with
  chocolate sauce you would:
  Don't you think this chocolate sauce thing has gone far enough? But to
  answer the question: if it was me, I'd untie her, hand her a towel, tell
  her to go have a shower and to not be embarassed, and then later on ask her
  out to dinner. Mulder would probably make a wisecrack and then find the
  mutant/aliens responsible.
  
  8.  Marital status?
  Blissfully single (at least that's what I keep telling myself.
  Self-delusion can go a long way).
  
  9.  Desperation status?
  It was getting pretty bad there for a while, but I had a torrid and steamy
  affair before Christmas and I feel a lot better now...
  
  10.  Other must-see shows?
  Simpsons, Mad About You, Seinfeld, Roseanne, Babylon 5, Yes Minister,
  Frontline and a new show on ABC Sunday nights, Taking Over The Asylum. A
  very good show.
  
  11.  What TV shows should they bring back?
  Quantum Leap, The Muppet Show (Channel Ten, are you listening?), Max
  Headroom, The Prisoner, The Tick, Monkey and of course, The Banana Splits
  Adventure Hour.
  Ooops, almost forgot Hong Kong Phooey.
  
  12.  What TV shows should be euthanased immediately.
  I'm glad you asked. It has always been a toss-up between "Are You Being
  Served" and "Full House". Every copy world-wide should be destroyed. I
  personally blame both of these shows for making me the lunatic I am today
  (not that I've ever watched them more than once, just knowing that they
  exist drives me crazy)
  
  13.  If you were Scully and you found Mulder naked you would:
  Laugh, take photos and sell them to fanatical American fanatics for
  exorbitant prices. (Did anyone else see someone trying to flog off a copy
  of RS for $75?)
  
  14.  How long have you been on the net?
  I've had permanent access since September '93, but was hanging around since
  the start of '92.
  
  "Now go away before I answer your questions a second time!"
  
  "But I live here," Annette protests.
  
  "Oh yeah," answers Matt with a shrug, "I forgot," and, pausing only to pick
  up his penguin, he exits through the door.
  
  Utterly exhausted, Annette collapses on the sofa, vaguely mumbling, "Never
  again.  Never ever ever again........"
  
  "Amen," says Intensity.
