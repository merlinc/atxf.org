  MAXWELL/INTENSITY ENTERPRISES PRESENT
  
  THE FIFTH PENGUIN REPORT
  
  Screenplay by Annette Fraser from the ideas formed in the warped brains of
  Intensity and Annette.
  
  
  TRANSPORTS OF DELIGHT
  
  It is an ordinary day in the hallowed halls of aus.tv.x-files.  The walls
  of the dimly-lit bar-room are lined with X-Files posters, copied magazine
  articles, trading cards, printouts of JPEGs, shelf after shelf full of
  videos, books, coffee mugs and baseball caps.  People dressed in X-Files
  t-shirts and expensive trenchcoats are lounging at tables, merrily chatting
  over coffee and doughnuts, showing each other their torches and
  occasionally emitting excited squeals as they come across a particularly
  sexy picture in one of the thousands of magazines strewn carelessly around
  the room.   From the jukebox in the corner comes the sound of a Vandals CD
  being played for the thousandth time today (in fact, itÕs the only CD in
  the jukebox).  Even Dregs is beginning to show signs of annoyance.
  Scattered randomly around the tables are Golden and Tarnished Xs, Pyrite
  Xs, Flaming Xs, Laminex Turtles, Crucified Gumbies and Petrified Penguins;
  each award inscribed with the relevant particulars.
  
  Defying all the known laws of chronological conversation, people speak out
  aloud, voicing their opinions on many and varied topics and occasionally,
  oh so occasionally, someone mentions The X-Files.  As a result of this, the
  denizenÕs skin tone almost imperceptibly turns a scant shade browner.  Many
  who have been here a long long time are looking decidedly red; SUNBURNt,
  you could say.  A yorkshiremen thread starts, continues for several minutes
  and then dies back into obscurity.
  
  Matt-5 stands up and says, "alfkgjakjfhakjhiujhkjh" which is greeted by a
  complete lack of response; Antti sits alone reading "Moominsummer Madness"
  for the nth time; a regular series of slurps can be heard over the murmur
  of conversation as Lyndal works her way through a bottle of chocolate
  topping; Steve Turtle watches her with morbid fascination and slowly shakes
  his head; Nina runs around merrily patting all brown-eyed men
  affectionately on the head;  Steve Leahy bemoans to no-one in particular
  his disappointment that no-one reads the FAQ; Ken, pen and notebook in
  hand, assiduously writes down everything anyone says in case he has to do a
  report on it; Andy follows Holly around on his knees plaintively pleading
  with her to lend him the video of 'Pusher'; every now and then a loud
  BWAHAHAHAHAHAHA followed by a string of expletives erupts from Dregs; on
  the periphery Rob Whyte speaks earnestly to an attentive table of newbies,
  loudly and clearly extolling the many virtues of Babylon 5;  Marcus moves
  around the tables offering camembert and water crackers to which most
  people respond ungratefully, "What?  No pizza?"; Waz repeats his claim that
  he's coming back, and is met by responses of, "Waz?  Waz who?"  from
  various newbies, who are mildly beaten up for their ignorance;  the
  Parmenter sisters sit together, hysterically laughing at nothing at all;
  Ralph the Wonder Llama, ecstatic at actually being there, tells anyone who
  will listen about the dearth of X-Philes in Hobart; Gene sits quietly,
  sipping an imported L&P and revelling in the sugar high; /Matt says
  nothing.
  
  >From out of the darkened corners of the room a lurker steps tentatively
  toward the bar, glances nervously to his left and right and, in a quavering
  voice but one filled with bravado, says to the barman, "Metamucil on the
  rocks.  Make it a double."  With one mighty gulp he drinks it, slams the
  empty glass onto the bar and turns to face the others.  Soon he finds
  himself the centre of attention, spouting drivel about anything and
  everything; replying to even the most mundane comments with little more
  than a "Ya-huh" or an "And I've never been the same again".  It is only
  when he notices his skin peeling away that he makes his cumbersome way back
  to the atxf reality and sits down sheepishly, this time at one of the
  tables near the middle of the room.
  
  The shaky status quo continues uninterrupted for quite a few minutes when
  an almost inaudible, high-pitched humming begins to impinge upon the
  collective eardrums of the crowd.  The noise level of general chit chat
  becomes more determined and rises with the humming.  Tubs of fresh vanilla
  ice cream suddenly materialise in the trembling hands of those present and
  an expression of abject fear crosses the faces of those who recognise these
  signs.  A shimmering light begins to glow in the centre of the bar and an
  earsplitting "FZAK!" tears through the air.  "FZAK!" it goes and from the
  now brain-searing iridescent glow materialise two relaxed and somewhat
  refined figures who step unconcernedly from the bar and into the room.
  
  "Ah!  We're here, yer Maj," says the intense one.
  
  "Yes, I can see that," Annette replies somewhat testily, as she removes her
  black kid gloves, "thankyou *so* much for letting me know, Intensity."
  
  "Settle down my Queen," replies Intensity,  "I'll go and get you a
  cappucino."
  
  Somewhat mollified the Queen replies, "Ah, that would be just lovely."  She
  smiles a smug smile, secure in the knowledge that a cappucino is on its
  way.
  
  A small, red-haired girl, laden down with at least fifty magazines, runs
  breathlessly up to Annette, nearly flooring her in her enthusiasm.  Annette
  reacts with a cringe and an involuntary step backwards as she stares with
  distaste at the visage before her.  The girl immediately begins to jump up
  and down rapidly in a tantrum demanding  in a high, sing-song voice,
  "Where's the new Penguin Report?  Where's the new Penguin Report?"
  
  Recognition finally creeps across Annette's overused brain cell.  "Holly,
  be quiet,"  she sighs tiredly as she sits down at a table,  "you don't know
  what it's like, the pressure of writing those things.  Having to put up
  with the mad, lunatic ravings of Intensity for a whole *week* as you think
  you finally have it written and then he throws in more and more ideas and
  more ways of fitting Nina into the story, and then tells you that he can't
  keep doing this.  It's moider!"  She raises her hands in mute appeal then
  lets them drop back into her lap.   "Besides," she concludes with a
  sour-faced sneer, "writing an X-Report seems to exclude you from receiving
  awards."
  
  Holly stares blankly at Annette.  "Sooooo this means what, exactly?"
  
  Annette sighs.  "Heavy sigh," she says wearily and puts on her
  explaining-to-a-child voice which gives it a syrupy quality with lots of
  significant pauses between the words.  "It means that *I* want an award,
  but that *I* can't get one because *I* keep writing the reports and no-one
  wants to nominate the writers, especially *I*, er *me*."  She smiles
  brightly at Holly, her face a perfect picture of insincerity.
  
  Holly continues to stare blankly, *her* face a perfect picture of
  incomprehension.
  
  Annette gives up.  "It's Penguin Report time, Holly.  You can go back to
  your seat now."
  
  "Yay!" Holly squeals, and skips back to her place, wide-eyed in
  anticipation.
  
  Intensity returns with the cappucino which Annette takes gratefully and
  from which she begins to sip rather thoughtfully.  However, she's not quite
  thoughtful enough to prevent the frothy milk moustache from forming on her
  upper lip.
  
  "Ah, your Majesty.  I see the influence of the male dominated Sydney
  gatherings has made you follically gifted," quips Daniel with a leer.
  
  "T'was witty, to be sure, Daniel,"  answers Annette, wiping her mouth with
  the back of her hand, "but hardly up to your usual double entendre
  standard.  However, your comment has just precluded you from winning any
  more Petrified Penguins.  So nyah!"  And with all the grace of a
  Merchandise Queen at a convention, Annette pokes her tongue out at Daniel
  and dances like a taunting child.
  
  As Daniel slinks away, highly embarrassed at the less than regal antics of
  the Queen, there is another loud "FZAK" and a blinding flash of light.  As
  the glow subsides and the various atxfers slowly regain their sight, they
  are greeted by the vision of Annette and Intensity standing at the bar,
  surrounded by several newly polished and inscribed Petrified Penguin Awards
  which have materialised beside them.  Annette holds a large piece of paper
  in her hand, turns, smiles condescendingly at Intensity, then turns back to
  face the members of the crowd who have now pulled their chairs closer and
  are waiting in breathless anticipation of the obviously impending awards
  ceremony.
  
  Annette clears her throat (several atxfers grimace in disgust) and begins
  to speak.  As she reads out the awards, blushing recipients push their way
  towards the bar where Intensity smiles, hands them their award and then
  quickly pushes them away before they can try making a speech.

  Intensity grins maniacally and clutches his Petrified Penguin to his chest.
   Annette continues.
  
  "The Oracle wins the Do-It-Yourself Home Improvement Become an FBI Agent by

  And the next award is...."
  
  Annette stops reading, frowns, peers at the piece of paper, turns it
  upside-down, frowns again and turns to Intensity.
  
  "What's this?" she asks jabbing at the list of awards with a pointy red
  fingernail.
  
  Feigning innocence (very badly I might add) Intensity pretends to look at
  the list.
  
  "Oh that," he says, "I put that in as a little surprise."  He takes the
  list, clears his throat and reads loudly;
  
  "The Mitch Pileggi Award for Being Annette Better Than Anyone Else goes to
  our own Merchandise Queen, Annette."
  
  He begins to applaud as Annette scowls and eyes him suspiciously.  Suddenly
  there is a loud squeal and a splatter of chocolate topping impacts with the
  backs of several heads as Lyndal stands, gapes, points and screams,
  "MITCH!!!!!!"
  
  There is absolute stunned silence as Mitch Pileggi appears from the rear of
  the room and makes his way past the staring crowd towards a completely
  flabbergasted and gobsmacked Merchandise Queen who somehow manages not to
  faint or have a narcoleptic nap attack.  He pauses, winks once at Lyndal
  (who drops her bottle of chocolate topping which smashes on the floor) then
  takes Annette by the hand and leads her towards the door.
  
  "Nooooooo!!!!!"  screams Lyndal in anguish, leaping over chairs, scrambling
  frantically over disgruntled atxfers and hurling herself at the two
  departing figures.  But just as she reaches the doorway, there is a burst
  of light and Annette and Mitch disappear.  A distraught Lyndal searches
  vainly for a few minutes then returns to her chair in dejection and sulks
  mightily, occasionally casting the Look of Death (TM) upon the hapless
  Intensity who continues handing out the awards.

  continues to kill him with her glare, takes any notice whatsoever.

   5.     Why do you like The X-Files?
  
  Because it's got an X in the title.  This is obviously the reason since by
  putting an X in the name of anything, marketers have immediately  given
  that product magical associative properties which make every X-Phile go out
  to buy/steal/drool over it.
  
  I also like it because they put in a good effort to make the show more
  polished than the regular crap which is crammed down our throats.  The fact
  that it is produced to such a high quality impresses me, in that there are
  at least some people who think that the viewing public are worth
  entertaining.
